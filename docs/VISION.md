# Vision

This document explains why The English Codex exists, who it is for, and what principles should guide the project.

## The problem

Learning English alone is not difficult because information is missing. It is difficult because information is everywhere, often disorganized, passive, and mixed with bad advice.

Self-directed learners usually face three problems:

1. **No clear path** — they jump between topics without building a foundation.
2. **Passive study** — watching and reading feel productive, but little stays.
3. **No memory of mistakes** — the same errors keep returning because they are not recorded, corrected, and reviewed.

The English Codex answers those problems with one ordered path, an active notebook method, and a habit of logging and reviewing errors.

## What the project is

The English Codex is a free, public, static website that teaches and organizes the core knowledge of English from A0 to C2.

The learner reads a page, understands it, copies the essential part by hand into a personal notebook, creates original examples, corrects mistakes, and reviews over time.

The website teaches. The handwritten codex consolidates. Practice turns knowledge into skill.

## What the project is not

- It is not a full course.
- It is not a magic fluency method.
- It is not a replacement for a teacher.
- It is not a replacement for official exam materials.
- It is not an app, platform, service, or account-based product.
- It does not collect personal data, require login, or depend on a backend.

## Who it is for

The project is for learners who want structure and method:

- children making first contact with English;
- teenagers studying for school, courses, or exams;
- adults improving English for work, travel, or study;
- older learners who want a clear and calm path;
- self-taught learners;
- Cambridge exam candidates;
- people who learn better by writing by hand.

The reader is treated as intelligent. Explanations must be simple enough for beginners, but never childish or patronizing.

## Guiding principles

### 1. Active over passive

Every page must give the learner something to do: copy, write, say aloud, correct, review, or apply.

### 2. One clear path

Content follows CEFR levels from A0 to C2. A learner should always know where they are and what comes next.

### 3. Honest and plain

No fake shortcuts, hype, or unrealistic promises. English improves through exposure, repetition, correction, and time.

### 4. Built for the notebook

The handwritten codex is the heart of the method. Every content page must make it obvious what the learner should write down.

### 5. Simple by design

This is a static website, not a platform. No database, accounts, ads, tracking, or unnecessary complexity.

### 6. Site-first, notebook-friendly

The website is the main product. The notebook is the study method. Printing is optional support, not the core experience.

### 7. Free and respectful

The project is free for non-commercial use and avoids copying protected images, logos, official materials, or long quotations.

### 8. Accessible

The site must use readable type, strong contrast, semantic structure, keyboard-friendly navigation, and a calm reading experience.

## What success looks like

The English Codex succeeds if a learner can:

1. open the site and know where to start;
2. study one page and produce a useful notebook entry;
3. follow the path from A0 to C2 without feeling lost;
4. review past pages and see real progress;
5. use the project without needing an account, payment, or powerful device.

If a feature does not support this, it does not belong in the project.
